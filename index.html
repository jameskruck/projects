Project Portfolio Overview

My project portfolio showcases my commitment to creating engaging, inclusive learning experiences that leverage sound pedagogical principles while embracing innovative technologies. Each project reflects my belief that thoughtful educational design can transform learning experiences, making them more accessible and effective for all students.

With experience spanning humanities and technical disciplines, I bridge traditional academic approaches with innovative digital environments. My work focuses on helping faculty implement evidence-based teaching strategies that support diverse learners.

The featured projects below represent a range of initiatives, from comprehensive course redesigns to faculty development workshops and digital learning tools. Each demonstrates my approach to integrating Universal Design for Learning principles with engaging technologies to create learning environments that adapt to student needs.

Featured Projects

Faculty Development AI in Education Educational Technology

GenAI in Teaching & Learning Workshop

An interactive four-part workshop that introduces faculty to generative AI in education. This project combines hands-on AI exploration with evidence-based teaching strategies and curriculum design principles, including practical implementation planning and authentic assessment approaches.

Developed in response to the rapid adoption of AI tools like ChatGPT by students (45% usage) compared to lower faculty adoption (15% usage), addressing the need for faculty support in understanding and implementing AI effectively.

Accessibility Inclusive Teaching Faculty Development

UDL Plus One Workshop

A practical approach to implementing Universal Design for Learning principles through incremental "Plus One" enhancements to existing courses. This workshop helps faculty identify simple but high-impact changes they can make to improve accessibility and engagement.

The workshop includes hands-on exercises for creating multiple means of representation, engagement, and expression, with a focus on digital content accessibility and assessment design.

Course Redesign: The Tragic Spirit

A comprehensive transformation of a traditional Classics course into an engaging, accessible digital learning experience. This project involved converting an in-person course into a blended format that seamlessly integrates asynchronous and synchronous learning elements.

The redesign focused on competency-based learning, incorporating retrieval practice, flexible assessment options, and multiple pathways for student engagement with classical texts and concepts.

Curriculum Design

My approach to curriculum design centers on creating learning experiences that are engaging, accessible, and aligned with clearly defined outcomes. Key areas of expertise include:

Outcome-Based Course Design

Developing courses with clear learning outcomes that drive assessment strategies and learning activities, ensuring constructive alignment throughout.

Authentic Assessment Design

Creating assessments that reflect real-world applications of knowledge and skills, providing meaningful feedback opportunities and promoting deeper learning.

Program Mapping

Aligning course outcomes with program-level competencies to ensure comprehensive curriculum coverage and progressive skill development.

Inclusive Curriculum Development

Integrating Universal Design for Learning principles to create flexible learning pathways that accommodate diverse learner needs and preferences.
